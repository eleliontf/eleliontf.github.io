About us

Who we are

We are a non-profit organization dedicated to empowering the less privileged, particularly the girl child. Our mission is to improve their social status through sustainable microcredit, literacy advocacy, training, research, collaboration, and community participation. We provide vocational skills training, small grants, and loans to support economic empowerment. Our team is passionate about creating a more equitable society, and we believe that every individual deserves the opportunity to thrive. We focus on promoting education, economic independence, and social justice. Through our programs, we aim to make a positive impact on the lives of those we serve, promoting a brighter future for all. We strive to be a catalyst for change, bridging the gap between resources and opportunities for the less privileged, and empowering them to break the cycle of poverty

What we do

We provide:

  • Sustainable microcredit to support economic empowerment
  • Literacy advocacy and training to promote education
  • Vocational skills training for women, youths, and adolescent girls
  • Research and collaboration to inform our programs
  • Community participation and engagement to drive social change
  • Networking opportunities among various stakeholders
  • Small grants and loans to support self-help women's groups
  • Collaboration with government, NGOs, and private donors to advance our mandate and other relevant women's development programs

Our goals

Our ultimate goal is to improve the social status of the less privileged and girl child through sustainable economic empowerment, education, and skills development, leading to a more equitable and just society.

CEO with the Women

The ETF hosted a program focused on empowering women for a brighter future, with a primary emphasis on Empowerment of the Girl child. The program delved into various aspects, including raising awareness about the significance of educating girls, showcasing notable women achievers in Nigeria, stressing the importance of financial preparedness for our children's future, advocating for better opportunities to secure a brighter future for girls, promoting family planning to ensure responsible parenthood, and advocating for equal opportunities for both male and female children.

Guest Speaker Session


Guest Speaker Session

Nurse Mrs. Faith Yusuf on Health and Family Well-being during the outreach program, Nurse Faith Yusuf, owner of Jordan Clinic, delivered a compelling session addressing women on critical health matters. She emphasized the importance of family planning, raising children we can adequately care for, the effects of unsafe abortion, and the vital role of sex education for our girl children. These topics align closely with ETF's mission to empower women and young girls through education, health awareness, and community engagement, ensuring they are equipped with the knowledge and resources needed to make informed decisions about their lives and future.

Pastor Janet Dikko

Pastor Janet Dikko The founder of El Elion Transformation Foundation (ETF), was driven by her passion for women and girl child empowerment, especially in a community where she observed that many women were idle, lacking opportunities for growth and self-reliance. This deeply concerned her and sparked her vision to create an organization that would provide women and girls with the tools, skills, and support they needed to transform their lives. Through ETF, she has dedicated herself to fostering education, economic empowerment, and social change, ensuring that every woman and girl has the chance to realize her full potential.

Mrs. Bekky Benson

Mrs. Bekky Benson is the Project Manager at El Elion Transformation Foundation (ETF). She studied Catering and Pastry at Amsterdam Science Park and attended The London School of English. At ETF, she has successfully led the Entrepreneurship Skills Training Program, empowering women and young girls with the knowledge and skills to start and sustain their own businesses. Her dedication and leadership continue to make a positive impact on the communities ETF serves.

Hannatu Dikko Ibrahim

Hannatu Dikko Ibrahim is the Program Coordinator at El Elion Transformation Foundation (ETF). She holds a BSc in Biology and has extensive experience in education, complemented by a certificate in NGO management. In her role, Hannatu plays a pivotal part in shaping and implementing the foundation’s programs. Before joining ETF, Hannatu worked as a Basic Science and Biology teacher, where she gained a deep understanding of the educational challenges faced by young girls. Her experience in education, combined with a strong passion for community service, naturally led her to ETF. She now oversees the development and execution of programs aimed at empowering women and children, making a significant impact in the communities ETF serves.

Favour Ibrahim

Favour Ibrahim serves as the Community Development Officer at El Elion Transformation Foundation (ETF). With a BSc degree in Community Medicine and Public Health From Bingham University Nigeria, Favour brings a wealth of experience and expertise to the organization. Favour's extensive background in community medicine has provided her with a profound understanding of public health issues and the strategies necessary to address them effectively. Prior to joining ETF, she worked with various public health initiatives and non-profit organizations, where she led projects focused on improving health outcomes, promoting environmental sustainability, and empowering underserved communities.
